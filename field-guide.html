Field Guide · Quick Start

Fourth Dynasty Constraint Engine

v4.1 · runs in any browser
no install · no internet · offline

Could Old Kingdom Egypt's attested supply capacity actually have built the six Fourth Dynasty pyramids on their historical timelines? Run it and see , under every published capacity estimate, the model still shows a shortfall against the historical timelines.

What a deficit means: it shows where a set of capacity estimates is inconsistent with the attested timeline , that the assumed rates were too low, not that the monuments were impossible to build. The engine tests the numbers, not the pyramids.

Core limestone Tura casing Aswan granite , one shared workforce, whether you run a single build or the full 101-year programme.

Reading the top cards

Acc. DeficitGap between demand and the tested capacity. Above zero = the assumed rates fall short.
Core UtilOver 100% = over-committed.
BottleneckThe subsystem that binds most. This is what to attack.
DurationsLongest Structure (one build) vs Programme Completion (the whole thing) , they differ, by design.

The seven tabs

1Deficit , the headline curve. Rising = demand outruns the tested rates.
2Demand/Cap , annual demand vs the ceiling.
3Bottlenecks , which subsystem fails, how often.
4Material Flow , core / casing / granite over time.
5Sensitivity , the ◀ KEY LEVER row is what to change.
6Reverse Solver , target a duration, get the rates it needs.
7Scenarios , save (persists) & compare side by side.

Presentation mode , for showing a room

Click PRESENT for oversized numbers and a plain-English status line , "Required throughput exceeds capacity by X Mt, with Y binding for Z years."

Just six key levers, the binding subsystem auto-highlighted, a live baseline → changed → consequence readout, and a one-click Orthodox Baseline reset. Drag a lever; the verdict updates as you talk.

Gotchas , Labour % must total 100 (it warns in red). Programme Demand should equal your entered masses; if it doesn't, a structure param is off. Pull the year-by-year table with ⬇ EXPORT CSV.

The two-minute test , reproduce the headline result

Load Lehner

The default. Note Accumulated Deficit ≈ 12.4 Mt.

Open Bottlenecks

See what's binding. Only two subsystems ever do: placement in 65 years, granite dressing in 9.

Add workers

Drag Peak Workforce to 100k. Deficit drops… then plateaus. Granite dressing and Tura quarrying set the floor, and neither scales with people.

Cannibalise

Toggle it on , the deficit gets worse. That's the finding.
